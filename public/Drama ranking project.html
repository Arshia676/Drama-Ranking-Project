Startup:


                                   

"Startup" is a 2020 South Korean drama series that delves into the world of technology startups through the intertwined lives of its main characters:

    Seo Dal-mi (Bae Suzy)

  • Seo Dal-mi (Bae Suzy) is a young woman with a dream of becoming a successful entrepreneur like Steve Jobs.
  • She navigates challenges with optimism and determination in both her personal and professional life.

    Nam Do-san (Nam Joo-hyuk)

  • Nam Do-san is the founder of Samsan Tech, a struggling startup known for its innovative ideas but facing numerous obstacles.
  • He is portrayed as a socially awkward genius who develops romantic feelings for Seo Dal-mi.

    Won In-jae (Kang Han-na)

  • Won In-jae is Dal-mi's older sister, characterized by her ambition and successful career at a prestigious company.
  • She shares a complex relationship with her family, particularly with Dal-mi.

    Han Ji-pyeong (Kim Seon-ho)

  • Han Ji-pyeong plays a pivotal role as a successful and stoic investor who becomes a mentor to both Seo Dal-mi and Nam Do-san.
  • His troubled past influences his interactions and adds depth to his character.

Ending (Spoiler Alert):

In the final episodes of "Startup," the characters undergo significant developments in their personal and professional lives. Seo Dal-mi, Nam Do-san, Won In-jae, and Han Ji-pyeong face pivotal moments that shape their futures.

Overall, "Startup" captivates me with its blend of hardwork,perserverance, drama, and entrepreneurship, offering insights into the complexities of pursuing dreams and navigating the competitive world of technology startups.

Be Yourself:

                                       

"Be Yourself" (also known as "Be Melodramatic") is a 2021 Chinese drama series directed by Li Zhi, which centers around the lives of four young women embarking on their university journey. Each character possesses unique traits and aspirations that shape their individual paths to self-discovery and independence.

    Xia Lang Lang (Shen Yue)

  • Xia Lang Lang (Shen Yue) starts off as tomboyish and logical but discovers her softer side and the possibility of romance as she navigates university life.

    • Huang Fu Shu Min (Zhang Ruo Nan)

  • Huang Fu Shu Min (Zhang Ruo Nan), who thrives on acting cute and dependent, learns the importance of independence and begins to forge her own path towards personal growth.

    • Fan Xiao Yu (Vivienne Tien)

  • Fan Xiao Yu (Vivienne Tien), an intelligent beauty from a wealthy family, initially adheres to familial expectations but finds freedom and potential romance through exploring the world of business with a fellow student.

    • Yang Jia Qian (Zhang Xin Yi)

  • Yang Jia Qian (Zhang Xin Yi), appearing mature beyond her years, confronts her hidden thoughts and feelings now that she's away from her family's influence.

Throughout the series, these women support each other through challenges, career aspirations, and personal relationships, demonstrating the complexities of modern life and the pursuit of individual identity.The drama concludes on a note of empowerment and self-discovery, as each character embraces their true selves and navigates towards fulfilling their dreams and desires amidst the trials of university and beyond.
